Sun Bears

The Sun Bear class is the afternoon session for 4- and 5-year old children. Classroom activities build on previous learning experiences, and children’s interests help drive projects and collaborative play. Teachers support each child’s readiness for kindergarten across all academic, social and emotional areas.

Classroom Goals

The nursery school ensures that our school environment is safe, healthy and conducive to children’s exploration and independence. Our teachers encourage students to:

  • Feel comfortable and secure, developing the understanding that learning is fun.
  • Respect themselves and others, focusing on promoting kindness, empathy and cooperation.
  • Use their natural wonder and curiosity to explore and make connections to the world around them.
  • Think and act more independently.
  • Feel successful about their accomplishments, big or small.
  • Grow socially, physically and cognitively.

Teachers

Colleen Corbett

AB English, Minor in Spanish, MAEd Counseling

Colleen Corbett begins her seventh year as a teacher at WUNS, teaming for the sixth year with Katie in the Sun Bear classroom. She also is the Spanish specialist, teaching once weekly lessons to all the classrooms. In addition, Colleen coordinates the morning Bear Tracks program and maintains the WUNS website. She worked in the student technology and training department for five years at Washington University prior to transferring to the nursery school in 2013.

Before coming to Washington University, she taught second grade for nine years at The College School. Her degrees include a Bachelor of Arts in English, with minors in Spanish, writing, and women’s studies, and a Master of Education, both attained from the University of Missouri. Additional graduate school hours in early childhood education and writing were attained at Webster University and Washington University in St. Louis. Her favorite activity with the children is sharing literature that connects to experiences in the classroom.

Colleen loves to travel and play in the outdoors with her wonderful, over-functioning but humble husband. They live in Webster Groves with their fur-daughter, a mutt named Sadie who thinks she’s a princess. Their biological daughter is finally launched–she is a resident physician at Cincinnati’s Children’s Hospital.

Katie Loynd

BS Elementary Education, MAEd Teaching,
Early Childhood Education

Katie is very excited to begin her fifth year at WUNS, in the 2019 – 2020 school year teaching with Chona in the Teddy Bear classroom (mornings) and Colleen in the Sun Bear classroom (afternoons). She has taught kindergarten, first and second grades in the Ladue School District for many years.

She attained her undergraduate degree in elementary education from Creighton University and her Master of Arts in teaching early education from Webster University. Katie is inspired by young children; she is constantly amazed by how they see the joy in simple things. One of her goals as a teacher is to provide an enriching environment that naturally fosters curiosity, engaging conversations, imaginative play, creative movement, and thoughtful expression. She believes WUNS is a special place because of its warm and welcoming energy and its unique location on a college campus.

Katie and her husband Mike are St. Louis natives. They have four children, Alli (21), Jake (19), Elizabeth (17) and Will (14) and a dog Murphy, “the best West Highland White Terrier ever.” In her free time, Katie enjoys hiking, travel, swimming and being with her family.
